Cyclone

A cyclone is a large-scale weather system characterized by strong rotating winds around a low-pressure center. It is also known as a hurricane or typhoon, depending on the region. Cyclones can cause extensive damage through high winds, storm surges, and heavy rainfall, leading to flooding and landslides. They form over warm ocean waters when specific atmospheric conditions are met. Early warning systems and preparedness measures are crucial to mitigate their impacts and protect lives and property.

Flood

Floods are natural disasters caused by excessive and rapid accumulation of water on land that is usually dry. They can result from heavy rainfall, snowmelt, storm surges, or the failure of dams and levees. Floods can lead to widespread damage to homes, infrastructure, and agricultural land, and they pose a significant threat to human life.Managing flood risk through proper land-use planning, early warning systems.

Earth Quake

An earthquake is a natural geophysical phenomenon caused by the sudden release of energy in the Earth's crust, resulting in seismic waves that shake the ground. It occurs due to tectonic plate movements, volcanic activity, or human-induced processes. The severity of an earthquake is measured on the Richter scale or the moment magnitude scale (Mw). Earthquakes can cause devastating damage to buildings, infrastructure, and landscapes, and can trigger secondary hazards such as tsunamis.

WildFire

A large, destructive fire that spreads quickly over woodland or brush

WildFire

Wildfire is a destructive and uncontrolled fire that spreads rapidly through vegetation in natural areas like forests, grasslands, and bushlands. It can be ignited by lightning strikes, human activities, or volcanic eruptions. Wildfires have devastating impacts on ecosystems, air quality, and human settlements, often leading to loss of wildlife, homes, and lives. Firefighting efforts involve specialized teams, aerial support, and strategic containment to mitigate their destructive effects and protect communities in their path.
